About Us

Bohol is the main island of Bohol Province, which also includes 75 smaller islands. The island lies southeast from Cebu Island and southwest of Leyte Island in the Central Visayas region. This oval-shaped island is the tenth largest of the Philippine archipelago.

  • Sightseeing in Chocolate Hills
  • Stand up paddle boarding in Loboc River
  • Visiting Bohol Habitat Conservation Conservatory

Bohol is a haven of tropical natural beauty. The coastline of the island is skimmed by gentle coves and white sand beaches. Bohol is well-known locally as a paradise for divers and snorkelers, though it's not as internationally famous as Boracay. Dolphin watching and whale watching tours are popular with both residents and visiting tourists.

We Boholano's welcome our visitors from different places around the world. We want you to enjoy and cherish every moment of your stay in here. Explore now!

  • 01November to April

    Bohol experiences short rains with a bearable climate. It is not recommended for visiting if travellers are planning to trek or hike, as the trails can get slippery and difficult. Also, this season includes Christmas in December, and Holy Week in April. This is when the main tourist spots are filled with locals. If tourists want to avoid people and congestion, as well as enjoy a not-too-hot atmosphere, this is the perfect time to visit.

  • 02 May to July

    This is when Bohol’s climate reaches its hottest, and the sun is out for the maximum amount of time. This is the most ideal time to visit if tourists are looking to experience and maximize the ocean and the beaches.

  • 03August to October

    These days have a steady mix of sunny and rainy weather. If travellers are planning to visit during this time, they have to be strategic about planning your activities around the weather.

  • 04May to June

    These are when the dolphins come out on some of the beaches. If you want to catch them, make sure to visit during this time and hope that luck is on your side.

Foods

Loomweavers of the province produces cloth from raffia. Also known is the province's delicacies. The famous delicacy is the Kalamay/Calamay . It is a sweet delicacy made of coconut milk, brown sugar and ground glutinous rice.

Adventures

Bohol is one of the best places where you can stay for weeks. But if you don't have a long time to spare, then three to four days should be enough to make the most of your Bohol itinerary.

Cultures

The Boholano culture is much like the culture of the Philippines. It is based on the majority population of Austronesian or Malayo- Polynesian people on the island.

Historical Places

Bohol is derived from the word Bo-ho or Bo-ol. The island was the seat of the first international treaty of peace and unity between the native king Datu Sikatuna and Spanish conquistador Miguel López de Legazpi on 16 March 1565
